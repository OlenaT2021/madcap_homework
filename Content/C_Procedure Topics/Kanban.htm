Kanban

Kanban is an built on a philosophy of continuous improvement, where work items are “pulled” from a product backlog into a steady flow of work. The framework is applied using Kanban boards, a form of visual project management. In a Kanban board, tasks—represented as cards—move through stages of work—represented as columns. That way, your team can see where work is in real-time.

Kanban is especially popular with product, engineering, and software development teams. But any team that wants to create a more dynamic, flexible workflow can use them.

4 core principles of Kanban

There are four core principles to help guide your team as you think about rolling out a Kanban framework:

  1. Apply Kanban to your current workflow or process..

    Start with what you do now - Kanban is flexible and can work with your team's core practices.

  2. Agree to pursue incremental change over time.

    Big, quick change can disrupt your team's flow and performance so give them time to evolve processes.

  3. Respect current processes, roles, and responsibilities.

    Kanban doesn't have built-in roles so adapt the methodology to your current team structure.

  4. Encourage acts of leadership at all levels.

    With Kanban, any team member can chime in or even take the lead.

Kanban best practices

The Kanban core principles help guide the team’s mentality when it approaches the Kanban workflow. To implement a Kanban process, follow these six practices to help the team continuously improve and achieve incremental growth—the core tenets of the Kanban framework.

  • Visualize your work to see it move through stages.

  • Limit work in progress so your team can focus on completing tasks.

  • Manage and improve your flow to always deliver relevant tasks or products.

  • Make process policies explicit so your team knows exactly how to implement Kanban.

  • Implement feedback loops from customer and your team.

  • Improve collaboratively and evolve experimentally always keeping continuous improvement top of mind.
